#  Teen Mental Health Analysis using Power BI

##  Project Overview

This project analyzes survey data from **1,200 teenagers aged 13–19** to identify factors associated with depression status.

Using **Power BI**, I cleaned the data, performed exploratory data analysis (EDA), built an interactive dashboard, and developed business recommendations to support data-driven mental health initiatives.

---

##  Business Problem

A mental health research institute wanted to understand how lifestyle habits, social media usage, and mental health indicators differ between teenagers classified as depressed and those who are not.

The goal was to transform raw survey data into actionable insights that can help schools and mental health organizations develop targeted intervention strategies.

##  Key Findings

- Teenagers classified as depressed spent **6.7 hours** per day on social media compared with **4.5 hours** among those not classified as depressed.
- Depressed teenagers slept **1.7 fewer hours** on average.
- Stress levels were substantially higher among depressed teenagers (**8 vs 5**).
- Anxiety levels were also higher (**9 vs 6**).
- Exercise and study hours showed little difference between the two groups.
- Female teenagers had a slightly higher depression rate than males.
- TikTok users had the highest proportion of teenagers classified as depressed among the platform categories.

---

##  Recommendations

- Promote healthy digital habits among teenagers.
- Encourage healthy sleep routines.
- Expand access to school-based mental health services.
- Implement stress management and anxiety awareness programs.
- Continue monitoring mental health trends using data-driven approaches.
